Hydraulic Safety

This document outlines required hydraulic safety practices for TitanX equipment platforms during operation, inspection, troubleshooting, maintenance, and service activities.

Hydraulic systems may contain high-pressure fluid capable of causing serious injury, equipment damage, or hazardous operational conditions if handled improperly.

Safety Objectives​

Hydraulic safety procedures are intended to:

  • Reduce hydraulic-related injury risk
  • Prevent unexpected equipment movement
  • Control hazardous pressure conditions
  • Improve safe maintenance practices
  • Reduce equipment damage during service

Hydraulic System Hazards​

TitanX hydraulic systems may present hazards including:

  • High-pressure fluid injection
  • Hydraulic hose rupture
  • Unexpected attachment movement
  • Stored hydraulic pressure
  • Fluid leaks
  • Component failure during operation

All personnel must follow approved hydraulic safety procedures before working near active hydraulic systems.

Required PPE​

Personnel working with hydraulic systems must wear:

PPE ItemRequirement
Safety GlassesRequired
Protective GlovesRequired
Steel-Toe FootwearRequired
Protective ClothingRecommended during fluid service
Face ShieldRecommended during high-pressure inspection

Additional PPE may be required depending on service conditions.

Hydraulic Safety Requirements​

Personnel must:

  • Depressurize hydraulic systems before service
  • Lower attachments safely before shutdown
  • Inspect hoses and fittings regularly
  • Replace damaged hydraulic components immediately
  • Avoid direct contact with active leaks
  • Follow lockout/tagout procedures during maintenance

Do not operate equipment with unresolved hydraulic leaks or pressure instability.

Hydraulic Inspection Guidelines​

Before operation or service:

  1. Inspect hydraulic hoses for wear or damage.
  2. Verify fittings remain secure.
  3. Inspect for active fluid leaks.
  4. Review hydraulic pressure indicators.
  5. Verify attachments remain stable during operation.

Hydraulic Leak Safety​

WARNING​

Never use hands to inspect active hydraulic leaks.

High-pressure hydraulic fluid may penetrate skin and require immediate medical treatment.

Use approved inspection methods and protective equipment when inspecting hydraulic systems.

Hydraulic Service Precautions​

Before hydraulic maintenance:

  • Shut down equipment fully
  • Release stored hydraulic pressure
  • Apply lockout/tagout procedures
  • Verify zero-energy state
  • Allow systems to cool when required

Do not disconnect hydraulic lines during active pressure conditions.

Escalation Guidance​

Immediately escalate:

  • Active hydraulic leaks
  • Hose rupture
  • Pressure instability
  • Hydraulic warning indicators
  • Unexpected attachment movement
  • Fluid injection injuries

Do not continue operation during unresolved hydraulic safety conditions.
