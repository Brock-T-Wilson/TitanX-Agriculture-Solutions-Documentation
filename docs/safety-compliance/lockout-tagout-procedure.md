# Lockout/Tagout Procedure

This procedure outlines the required lockout/tagout (LOTO) process for TitanX equipment platforms during maintenance, inspection, troubleshooting, or service activities.

Lockout/tagout procedures help prevent accidental equipment startup, unexpected energy release, and hazardous equipment movement during service operations.

## Procedure Objectives

Lockout/tagout procedures are intended to:

- Protect personnel during equipment service
- Prevent accidental equipment startup
- Control hazardous energy sources
- Reduce injury risk during maintenance
- Support operational safety compliance

## Required Safety Precautions

Before beginning lockout/tagout procedures:

- Park equipment on stable terrain
- Shut down equipment fully
- Lower all attachments safely
- Engage parking brake
- Wear required PPE

:::warning
Failure to isolate hazardous energy sources before service may result in serious injury, equipment damage, or fatality.
:::

## Hazardous Energy Sources

TitanX equipment platforms may contain hazardous energy sources including:

- Electrical systems
- Hydraulic pressure
- Mechanical movement
- PTO-driven components
- Stored system pressure
- Engine startup systems

All hazardous energy sources must be isolated before service work begins.

## Required PPE

Personnel performing lockout/tagout procedures must wear:

- Safety glasses
- Protective gloves
- Steel-toe footwear
- Additional PPE required for service environment

## Lockout/Tagout Procedure

### 1. Notify Affected Personnel

Before service:

- Inform all affected personnel
- Identify equipment being removed from operation
- Communicate expected service duration
- Restrict unauthorized equipment access

---

### 2. Shut Down Equipment

Perform standard equipment shutdown procedures.

Verify:

- Engine operation has stopped
- PTO systems are disengaged
- Hydraulic systems are inactive
- Equipment movement has stopped completely

---

### 3. Isolate Energy Sources

Disconnect or isolate all hazardous energy sources including:

- Battery power systems
- Hydraulic pressure systems
- PTO driveline systems
- Mechanical movement systems

Follow all equipment-specific isolation procedures.

---

### 4. Apply Lockout Devices

Install approved lockout devices on isolated systems.

Verify:

- Devices are secured properly
- Energy isolation remains active
- Unauthorized startup is prevented

Only authorized personnel may apply or remove lockout devices.

---

### 5. Apply Tagout Identification

Attach approved tagout labels identifying:

- Authorized technician
- Service activity
- Lockout date
- Equipment status

Tagout information must remain visible during all service procedures.

---

### 6. Verify Zero Energy State

Before service work begins:

- Attempt equipment startup safely
- Verify systems remain inactive
- Confirm hydraulic pressure is released
- Verify no stored mechanical movement exists

Do not begin service until zero energy state is confirmed fully.

---

### 7. Complete Service Work

Perform approved inspection, maintenance, or troubleshooting procedures according to TitanX service documentation.

---

### 8. Remove Lockout/Tagout Devices

After service completion:

- Verify service work is complete
- Confirm personnel are clear of equipment
- Remove lockout devices safely
- Restore operational systems gradually

Only authorized personnel may remove applied lockout devices.

## Escalation Guidance

Immediately escalate:

- Failure to isolate hazardous energy
- Damaged lockout equipment
- Unexpected equipment startup
- Hydraulic pressure instability
- Unsafe service conditions

Do not continue service activities during unresolved hazardous conditions.
