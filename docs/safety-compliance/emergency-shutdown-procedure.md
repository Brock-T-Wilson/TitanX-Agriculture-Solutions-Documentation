# Emergency Shutdown Procedure

This procedure outlines the required emergency shutdown process for TitanX equipment platforms during unsafe operating conditions, equipment malfunction, or hazardous incidents.

Emergency shutdown procedures help reduce injury risk, limit equipment damage, and stabilize unsafe operational conditions.

## Procedure Objectives

Emergency shutdown procedures are intended to:

- Stop unsafe equipment operation quickly
- Reduce hazardous equipment movement
- Protect operators and nearby personnel
- Limit equipment damage
- Stabilize emergency situations

## Conditions Requiring Emergency Shutdown

Immediately initiate emergency shutdown if any of the following occur:

- Hydraulic system failure
- Active fire or smoke conditions
- Uncontrolled equipment movement
- PTO component failure
- Structural equipment damage
- Electrical arcing or burning odor
- Severe fluid leaks
- Loss of steering or braking control
- Operator injury or medical emergency

## Required Safety Precautions

During emergency shutdown:

- Prioritize personnel safety
- Maintain awareness of surrounding hazards
- Avoid unsafe equipment contact
- Move personnel away from hazardous areas when possible

:::warning
Do not continue equipment operation during active emergency conditions or unresolved safety hazards.
:::

## Emergency Shutdown Procedure

### 1. Stop Equipment Movement

Immediately reduce equipment movement safely.

Verify:

- Throttle is reduced to idle
- Equipment speed decreases safely
- Steering remains controlled
- Nearby personnel are clear of danger zones

Avoid abrupt movement when terrain conditions create rollover or collision risk.

---

### 2. Disengage PTO Systems

Immediately disengage all PTO-driven systems and attachments.

Verify:

- Attachment movement stops
- PTO indicators deactivate
- Driveline systems are inactive

---

### 3. Lower Attachments Safely

Lower all active attachments to ground level when safe to do so.

Do not remain beneath suspended equipment components.

---

### 4. Engage Parking Brake

Bring equipment to a complete stop and engage parking brake systems fully.

---

### 5. Shut Down Engine

1. Turn ignition controls to shutdown position.
2. Verify engine operation stops fully.
3. Remove ignition key when applicable.

If electrical failure prevents standard shutdown, follow equipment-specific emergency shutdown guidance.

---

### 6. Evacuate Unsafe Area if Required

Immediately evacuate the operational area if:

- Fire conditions exist
- Hydraulic rupture occurs
- Electrical hazards remain active
- Structural instability is present

Move personnel to a safe distance from equipment hazards.

---

### 7. Report Emergency Condition

Immediately notify:

- Supervisors
- Safety personnel
- Service technicians
- Emergency response teams when required

Provide clear details regarding:

- Equipment involved
- Active hazards
- Personnel injuries
- Operational conditions

## Post-Incident Requirements

After emergency shutdown:

- Do not restart equipment
- Restrict unauthorized access
- Complete required inspections
- Document incident details
- Await authorized service evaluation

Only qualified personnel may approve equipment return to operation.

## Escalation Guidance

Immediately escalate:

- Operator injuries
- Fire conditions
- Hydraulic system rupture
- Electrical hazards
- Structural equipment damage
- Environmental hazards

Do not resume operation during unresolved emergency conditions.
