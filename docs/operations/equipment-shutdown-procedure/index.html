Equipment Shutdown Procedure

This procedure outlines the standard shutdown process for TitanX agricultural equipment platforms following operational use.

Operators must follow all shutdown requirements to reduce equipment damage, maintain operational safety, and prepare equipment for inspection, maintenance, or future operation.

Shutdown Objectives​

The shutdown process is intended to:

  • Safely power down equipment systems
  • Reduce hydraulic and PTO-related risks
  • Prevent unintended equipment movement
  • Protect operational components
  • Prepare equipment for inspection or service

Required Conditions​

Before shutdown:

  • Equipment must be positioned on stable terrain.
  • Attachments must be lowered safely.
  • PTO systems must be disengaged.
  • Equipment movement must be fully stopped.
  • Parking brake must remain engaged.

Shutdown Procedure​

1. Move Equipment to Safe Position​

Position equipment in an approved shutdown location.

Verify:

  • Terrain is stable
  • Clearance space is adequate
  • Equipment does not obstruct operational traffic
  • Attachments are lowered fully

2. Disengage Operational Systems​

Before engine shutdown:

  • Disengage PTO systems
  • Return throttle to idle position
  • Disable attachment controls
  • Reduce hydraulic load gradually

Allow systems to stabilize before continuing shutdown.

3. Engage Parking Brake​

Verify the parking brake is fully engaged before shutdown.

Do not leave equipment unattended without confirming brake engagement.

4. Allow Engine Cooldown​

Allow the engine to idle briefly before shutdown to stabilize operating temperatures.

Cooldown duration may vary depending on:

  • Operational workload
  • Ambient temperature
  • Hydraulic system usage

Do not immediately shut down heavily loaded equipment systems.

5. Shut Down Engine​

  1. Turn ignition to shutdown position.
  2. Verify engine operation has fully stopped.
  3. Remove ignition key if applicable.
  4. Confirm operational displays power down correctly.

6. Depressurize Hydraulic Systems​

After shutdown:

  • Release residual hydraulic pressure
  • Return hydraulic controls to neutral position
  • Inspect for visible leaks or damage
warning

Hydraulic systems may remain pressurized after shutdown. Follow all pressure release procedures before inspection or service.

7. Complete Post-Operation Walkaround​

Inspect equipment for:

  • Fluid leaks
  • Attachment damage
  • Loose hardware
  • Active warning indicators
  • Tire or track damage

Report unresolved operational issues according to TitanX service procedures.

Shutdown Escalation Guidance​

If abnormal conditions are identified during shutdown:

  • Stop equipment operation immediately
  • Report the issue to the appropriate supervisor
  • Escalate unresolved faults to qualified service personnel

Do not return equipment to operation until identified faults are resolved.
