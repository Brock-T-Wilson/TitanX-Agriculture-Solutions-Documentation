# Shift Handoff Procedure

This procedure outlines the standard process for transferring operational equipment status, active work information, and unresolved issues between outgoing and incoming personnel.

Proper shift handoff procedures improve communication, reduce operational interruptions, and help maintain safe equipment operation across multiple shifts.

## Shift Handoff Objectives

The shift handoff process is intended to:

- Communicate equipment status clearly
- Identify unresolved operational issues
- Reduce startup confusion between shifts
- Improve operational consistency
- Support safe equipment operation

## Required Handoff Information

Outgoing personnel must communicate:

- Current equipment status
- Active warning indicators
- Maintenance concerns
- Equipment shutdown status
- Fuel and fluid conditions
- Operational delays or restrictions
- Outstanding troubleshooting issues

## Handoff Procedure

### 1. Review Equipment Status

Outgoing personnel must provide:

- Equipment currently in operation
- Equipment removed from service
- Equipment pending maintenance
- Equipment with active operational restrictions

Do not assume incoming personnel are aware of unresolved operational issues.

---

### 2. Communicate Active Faults

Review all unresolved:

- Hydraulic issues
- PTO-related faults
- Startup concerns
- Electrical warnings
- Navigation or GPS issues
- Safety concerns

Include any troubleshooting actions already completed.

---

### 3. Review Maintenance Conditions

Communicate:

- Upcoming maintenance requirements
- Active service schedules
- Fluid replacement needs
- Inspection failures
- Equipment awaiting technician review

---

### 4. Verify Equipment Shutdown Status

Confirm all inactive equipment has been:

- Shut down properly
- Parked in approved locations
- Secured safely
- Inspected for visible damage

Verify parking brake engagement and attachment positioning.

---

### 5. Transfer Operational Documentation

Ensure incoming personnel have access to:

- Equipment procedures
- Inspection checklists
- Maintenance schedules
- Active troubleshooting documentation
- Safety notices
- Service escalation information

---

### 6. Confirm Handoff Completion

Incoming personnel should verify:

- Information is understood clearly
- Outstanding issues are documented
- Equipment restrictions are acknowledged
- Safety concerns are communicated fully

## Escalation Guidance

Immediately escalate:

- Unresolved safety concerns
- Equipment damage
- Hydraulic leaks
- Active fault indicators
- Startup failures
- Operational hazards

Do not return unsafe equipment to service.
