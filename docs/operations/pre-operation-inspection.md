# Pre-Operation Inspection

Pre-operation inspections help identify safety concerns, equipment faults, and operational issues before equipment startup and field use.

All TitanX equipment operators must complete the required inspection process before beginning operation.

Failure to complete the inspection may result in equipment damage, operational failure, or safety risks.

## Inspection Objectives

The inspection process is intended to:

- Verify equipment readiness
- Identify visible equipment damage
- Confirm fluid and fuel levels
- Detect active warning indicators
- Reduce operational downtime
- Improve operator safety

## Required PPE

Operators performing inspections must wear:

- Safety glasses
- Protective gloves
- Steel-toe footwear
- High-visibility vest during active field operations

## Inspection Procedure

### 1. Perform Exterior Walkaround

Inspect the equipment exterior for:

- Visible structural damage
- Fluid leaks
- Loose hardware
- Damaged hoses or fittings
- Tire or track condition
- Attachment security

Do not operate equipment with unresolved structural or hydraulic damage.

---

### 2. Verify Fluid Levels

Inspect and confirm acceptable levels for:

- Engine oil
- Hydraulic fluid
- Coolant
- Fuel
- Transmission fluid

Refill fluids according to equipment maintenance specifications when required.

---

### 3. Inspect Hydraulic Components

Verify that:

- Hydraulic hoses are secure
- No active leaks are present
- Hydraulic connections are undamaged
- Pressure indicators are within acceptable ranges

:::warning
Hydraulic systems may remain pressurized after shutdown. Follow all depressurization procedures before inspection or service.
:::

---

### 4. Verify PTO & Attachment Systems

Inspect all PTO and attachment connection points.

Verify:

- PTO shields are installed
- Attachment hardware is secure
- No visible driveline damage exists
- PTO controls are disengaged before startup

---

### 5. Review Warning Indicators

Before startup:

- Review dashboard warning indicators
- Confirm active fault alerts are resolved
- Verify navigation and operational displays are functional

Do not begin operation with unresolved critical warnings.

---

### 6. Confirm Operational Area Safety

Before equipment movement:

- Verify the operational area is clear
- Confirm visibility conditions are acceptable
- Remove unnecessary personnel from the operating zone
- Review terrain conditions

## Inspection Completion

Operators must report unresolved inspection failures to the appropriate supervisor or service technician before equipment operation.
