# Equipment Startup Procedure

This procedure outlines the standard startup process for TitanX agricultural equipment platforms following completion of the required pre-operation inspection.

Operators must complete all inspection and safety requirements before initiating equipment startup.

## Startup Objectives

The startup process is intended to:

- Verify operational readiness
- Reduce equipment startup failures
- Identify active fault conditions
- Confirm system functionality
- Ensure safe equipment operation

## Required Conditions

Before startup:

- The pre-operation inspection must be completed.
- Required PPE must be worn.
- The operational area must be clear of hazards.
- All attachments must be secured properly.
- PTO systems must remain disengaged.

## Startup Procedure

### 1. Enter Operator Station

Verify:

- Operator seating position is secure
- Controls are unobstructed
- Visibility conditions are acceptable
- Parking brake is engaged

Adjust mirrors, seating, and display systems as required.

---

### 2. Verify System Status

Before ignition:

- Review dashboard indicators
- Confirm no active critical warnings are displayed
- Verify hydraulic pressure indicators are within acceptable range
- Confirm PTO systems remain disengaged

Do not continue startup if unresolved fault indicators are active.

---

### 3. Initiate Ignition Sequence

1. Insert ignition key or activate startup control.
2. Turn ignition to system power position.
3. Allow operational systems to initialize fully.
4. Start engine according to equipment startup controls.

Do not repeatedly cycle ignition during startup failures.

---

### 4. Monitor Startup Indicators

After engine startup:

- Verify stable engine idle
- Monitor hydraulic pressure indicators
- Confirm battery charging status
- Review temperature indicators
- Verify operational displays initialize correctly

If abnormal vibration, warning alerts, or unstable engine conditions occur, shut down equipment immediately.

---

### 5. Allow Equipment Warm-Up

Allow equipment systems to stabilize before operation.

Warm-up duration may vary depending on:

- Ambient temperature
- Hydraulic conditions
- Equipment workload requirements

Do not apply heavy operational load during initial warm-up.

---

### 6. Verify Operational Readiness

Before equipment movement:

- Release parking brake
- Verify steering response
- Confirm brake functionality
- Test low-speed movement
- Verify attachment controls respond correctly

## Startup Failure Guidance

If equipment fails to start:

1. Stop the startup attempt.
2. Review active warning indicators.
3. Inspect fuel and battery conditions.
4. Escalate unresolved faults to qualified service personnel.
