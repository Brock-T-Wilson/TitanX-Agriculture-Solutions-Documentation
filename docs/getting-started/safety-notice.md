# Safety Notice

This documentation portal contains operational, maintenance, troubleshooting, and service procedures for TitanX equipment platforms.

All personnel must review and follow applicable safety procedures before operating, inspecting, troubleshooting, or servicing equipment.

Failure to follow approved safety procedures may result in equipment damage, operational hazards, serious injury, or fatality.

## Safety Objectives

TitanX safety procedures are intended to:

- Reduce workplace injury risk
- Improve operational awareness
- Control hazardous energy sources
- Support safe equipment operation
- Standardize maintenance safety practices
- Reduce equipment-related incidents

## General Safety Requirements

Before operating or servicing equipment:

- Complete required training
- Wear all required PPE
- Perform required inspections
- Review operational procedures
- Verify work areas are safe
- Follow approved lockout/tagout procedures when applicable

Do not operate equipment during unresolved safety conditions.

## Hazard Awareness

Personnel must remain aware of hazards including:

| Hazard Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Hydraulic Hazards | High-pressure fluid, hose rupture |
| Mechanical Hazards | Moving components, PTO systems |
| Electrical Hazards | Damaged wiring, electrical faults |
| Operational Hazards | Unstable terrain, visibility limitations |
| Environmental Hazards | Extreme weather, contaminated work areas |

Additional hazards may exist depending on equipment configuration and operational environment.

## Emergency Conditions

Immediately stop equipment operation if any of the following occur:

- Hydraulic system failure
- Fire or smoke conditions
- Structural equipment damage
- Loss of steering or braking control
- Electrical arcing
- Active fluid leaks
- Unsafe equipment movement

Follow approved emergency shutdown procedures during active hazardous conditions.

## Required Safety Practices

Personnel must:

1. Follow approved operating procedures.
2. Use required PPE at all times.
3. Report unsafe conditions immediately.
4. Remove damaged equipment from service.
5. Follow maintenance safety procedures.
6. Maintain awareness of surrounding personnel and hazards.

---

## Safety Escalation Guidance

Immediately escalate:

- Operator injuries
- Active safety hazards
- Hydraulic rupture conditions
- Fire-related incidents
- Structural equipment damage
- Unsafe operational procedures

Do not resume operation during unresolved hazardous conditions.

## Documentation Responsibility

All personnel are responsible for:

- Following approved procedures
- Reporting unsafe conditions
- Identifying documentation concerns
- Maintaining operational awareness
- Supporting safe equipment operation
