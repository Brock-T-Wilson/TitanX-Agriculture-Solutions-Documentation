# Engine Does Not Start

This troubleshooting procedure provides diagnostic guidance for TitanX equipment platforms that fail to start during standard startup procedures.

Use this process to identify common startup-related conditions before escalating the issue for advanced service support.

## Symptoms

Operators may experience one or more of the following conditions:

- Engine does not crank
- Ignition systems activate without engine startup
- Repeated startup failure
- Clicking sound during ignition
- Dashboard power without engine response
- Startup interruption warnings

## Possible Causes

Common causes include:

- Low battery voltage
- Fuel delivery interruption
- Faulty starter relay
- PTO engagement during startup
- Active fault indicators
- Hydraulic system restrictions
- Electrical connection failure

## Required Safety Precautions

Before troubleshooting:

- Park equipment on stable terrain
- Engage parking brake
- Disengage PTO systems
- Wear required PPE
- Remove unnecessary personnel from operational area

:::warning
Do not repeatedly cycle the ignition system during startup failure conditions. Repeated attempts may damage electrical or starter system components.
:::

## Diagnostic Procedure

### 1. Verify Battery Condition

Inspect battery systems for:

- Loose terminal connections
- Corrosion buildup
- Low voltage conditions
- Damaged cables

If battery voltage is below acceptable operating range, recharge or replace the battery according to service procedures.

---

### 2. Confirm PTO Systems Are Disengaged

Verify all PTO systems remain disengaged before startup.

Some TitanX equipment platforms may prevent ignition while PTO systems are active.

---

### 3. Verify Fuel Levels

Inspect fuel conditions and confirm:

- Adequate fuel level
- No visible fuel leaks
- Fuel shutoff valves are open
- Fuel delivery systems are functioning normally

---

### 4. Review Active Warning Indicators

Inspect the operational display for:

- Fault indicators
- Electrical warnings
- Hydraulic alerts
- Safety lockout notifications

Document all active warning conditions before continuing troubleshooting.

---

### 5. Inspect Starter System Response

During startup attempt:

- Listen for clicking sounds
- Verify starter engagement
- Monitor dashboard voltage response

If the starter does not engage properly, escalate the issue for electrical system inspection.

---

### 6. Verify Hydraulic System Status

Some startup failures may occur if hydraulic systems remain in abnormal pressure conditions.

Inspect for:

- Active hydraulic warnings
- Visible hydraulic leaks
- Pressure-related fault indicators

---

## Escalation Guidance

Escalate the issue to qualified service personnel if:

- Startup failure remains unresolved
- Active electrical faults are present
- Hydraulic warnings remain active
- Starter components fail repeatedly
- Fuel delivery issues cannot be resolved

Do not continue startup attempts during unresolved fault conditions.
