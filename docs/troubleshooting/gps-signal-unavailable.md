# GPS Signal Unavailable

This troubleshooting procedure provides diagnostic guidance for TitanNav Guidance System conditions where GPS connectivity or positioning signals become unavailable during equipment operation.

GPS signal interruptions may affect field navigation, route alignment, operational mapping, and positioning accuracy.

## Symptoms

Operators may experience one or more of the following conditions:

- GPS signal loss warning
- Navigation interruption alerts
- Inaccurate positioning data
- Route alignment failure
- Operational mapping interruptions
- Signal instability during operation

## Possible Causes

Common causes include:

- Weak satellite connectivity
- Antenna obstruction
- Damaged GPS receiver hardware
- Connectivity module failure
- Environmental interference
- Software synchronization issues
- Power interruption to the TitanNav system

## Required Safety Precautions

Before troubleshooting:

- Move equipment to a safe operating location
- Reduce equipment speed if navigation becomes unstable
- Maintain visual awareness during operation
- Wear required PPE

:::warning
Operators must maintain safe equipment control at all times. TitanNav guidance features are intended to assist operation and do not replace operator awareness.
:::

## Diagnostic Procedure

### 1. Verify System Power Status

Confirm the TitanNav system powers on correctly.

Inspect for:

- Display startup failures
- Screen interruption
- Connectivity alerts
- Power-related warning indicators

Verify system power connections remain secure.

---

### 2. Inspect GPS Antenna Condition

Inspect GPS antenna components for:

- Physical damage
- Loose mounting hardware
- Obstructions
- Damaged cables
- Contamination buildup

Verify the antenna has clear visibility to open sky conditions.

---

### 3. Verify Satellite Signal Availability

Review navigation display indicators for:

- Signal strength
- Active satellite connections
- Positioning accuracy
- Connectivity status

Temporary signal interruptions may occur during severe environmental conditions or terrain obstruction.

---

### 4. Restart Navigation System

If signal instability continues:

1. Shut down the TitanNav system safely.
2. Allow the system to fully power down.
3. Restart the navigation platform.
4. Verify signal restoration after reboot.

Do not repeatedly restart systems during active electrical faults.

---

### 5. Verify Connectivity Module Status

Inspect wireless connectivity systems for:

- Cellular communication status
- Wi-Fi synchronization
- Software update interruption
- Active network warnings

Some operational mapping features may require stable connectivity.

---

### 6. Review Active Warning Indicators

Inspect operational displays for:

- GPS-related alerts
- Electrical warnings
- Connectivity faults
- Sensor communication failures

Document all active warning conditions before escalation.

## Escalation Guidance

Escalate the issue to qualified service personnel if:

- GPS signal loss remains unresolved
- Navigation instability continues
- Antenna damage is identified
- Connectivity modules fail repeatedly
- Positioning accuracy remains unstable

Do not rely on unstable navigation guidance during active field operations.
