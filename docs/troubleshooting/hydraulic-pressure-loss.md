## Symptoms

Operators may experience one or more of the following conditions:

- Slow hydraulic response
- Reduced lifting capability
- Attachment movement failure
- Hydraulic warning indicators
- Unstable steering response
- Abnormal hydraulic noise
- Loss of implement control

## Possible Causes

Common causes include:

- Low hydraulic fluid levels
- Hydraulic fluid contamination
- Damaged hydraulic hoses
- Active hydraulic leaks
- Pump restriction or failure
- Blocked hydraulic filters
- Pressure sensor malfunction

## Required Safety Precautions

Before troubleshooting:

- Park equipment on stable terrain
- Lower all attachments safely
- Shut down equipment properly
- Depressurize hydraulic systems
- Wear required PPE

:::warning
Hydraulic systems may remain pressurized after equipment shutdown. Failure to release stored pressure before inspection or service may result in serious injury.
:::

## Diagnostic Procedure

### 1. Verify Hydraulic Fluid Levels

Inspect hydraulic reservoirs and verify fluid levels are within acceptable operating range.

Inspect for:

- Low fluid conditions
- Fluid discoloration
- Visible contamination
- Burnt fluid odor

Refill or replace hydraulic fluid according to TitanX maintenance procedures when required.

---

### 2. Inspect for Hydraulic Leaks

Inspect all hydraulic components for:

- Damaged hoses
- Loose fittings
- Active leaks
- Fluid accumulation
- Damaged seals

Do not continue operation if active hydraulic leaks are identified.

---

### 3. Verify Hydraulic Filter Condition

Inspect hydraulic filters for:

- Restriction indicators
- Excessive contamination
- Improper installation
- Service interval expiration

Replace filters according to maintenance schedules when required.

---

### 4. Monitor Hydraulic Pressure Indicators

Review operational displays and pressure indicators for:

- Low pressure warnings
- Pressure fluctuation
- Sensor-related alerts
- System fault indicators

Document all active hydraulic warnings before escalation.

---

### 5. Inspect Hydraulic Pump Operation

Verify hydraulic pump systems operate normally during startup and operation.

Inspect for:

- Abnormal vibration
- Excessive noise
- Delayed pressure response
- Irregular fluid movement

Escalate unresolved pump-related concerns to qualified service personnel.

---

### 6. Verify Attachment Operation

Test hydraulic attachment functionality at low operational load.

Verify:

- Stable movement response
- Consistent pressure operation
- Proper implement control
- No abnormal vibration or noise

Stop operation immediately if hydraulic instability continues.

## Escalation Guidance

Escalate the issue to qualified service personnel if:

- Hydraulic pressure remains unstable
- Active leaks cannot be resolved
- Pressure warnings remain active
- Pump failure is suspected
- Hydraulic response continues degrading

Do not operate equipment during unresolved hydraulic fault conditions.
