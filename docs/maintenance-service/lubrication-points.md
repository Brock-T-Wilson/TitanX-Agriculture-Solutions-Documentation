# Lubrication Points

This procedure outlines standard lubrication requirements for TitanX equipment platforms.

Routine lubrication helps reduce component wear, improve equipment performance, and extend operational lifespan across moving mechanical systems.

## Service Objectives

Lubrication procedures are intended to:

- Reduce friction and wear
- Protect moving components
- Improve operational reliability
- Reduce overheating conditions
- Extend equipment service life

## Common Lubrication Areas

TitanX equipment platforms may include lubrication points for:

- PTO driveline components
- Steering linkage assemblies
- Suspension components
- Hydraulic attachment pivots
- Hitch assemblies
- Loader pivot points
- Chassis articulation points

Lubrication requirements may vary depending on equipment configuration and operational workload.

## Required Safety Precautions

Before performing lubrication procedures:

- Park equipment on stable terrain
- Shut down equipment fully
- Engage parking brake
- Lower attachments safely
- Wear required PPE

:::warning
Do not lubricate moving or energized equipment components during active operation.
:::

## Required PPE

Technicians performing lubrication procedures must wear:

- Safety glasses
- Protective gloves
- Steel-toe footwear

## Required Materials

Verify availability of:

- Approved TitanX lubricants
- Grease gun or lubrication equipment
- Shop towels
- Cleaning materials
- Service documentation

Use only approved lubricants specified for TitanX equipment platforms.

## Lubrication Procedure

### 1. Identify Lubrication Points

Inspect equipment documentation and identify all required lubrication locations.

Verify:

- Correct equipment model
- Proper lubrication intervals
- Approved lubricant type
- Accessible service points

Do not apply incorrect lubricant types to equipment components.

---

### 2. Clean Service Areas

Before lubrication:

- Remove dirt and debris
- Clean grease fittings
- Inspect surrounding components
- Verify no visible damage exists

Contaminated lubrication points may introduce debris into equipment systems.

---

### 3. Apply Lubricant

Apply lubricant slowly and evenly to required service points.

Inspect for:

- Proper lubricant flow
- Excessive resistance
- Damaged grease fittings
- Visible component wear

Do not over-lubricate components.

---

### 4. Inspect Moving Components

After lubrication:

- Verify smooth component movement
- Inspect pivot points
- Check for abnormal looseness
- Monitor for damaged hardware

Escalate excessive wear or abnormal movement conditions for advanced service inspection.

---

### 5. Remove Excess Lubricant

Clean excess lubricant from surrounding areas to reduce contamination buildup and improve inspection visibility.

---

### 6. Document Service Completion

Record:

- Service date
- Equipment hours
- Lubrication points serviced
- Lubricant type used
- Abnormal inspection findings

## Escalation Guidance

Escalate equipment for advanced service if:

- Lubrication fittings are damaged
- Components show excessive wear
- Pivot systems remain unstable
- Abnormal vibration continues
- Structural damage is identified

Do not continue operation during unresolved mechanical fault conditions.
