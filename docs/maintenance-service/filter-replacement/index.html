Filter Replacement

This procedure outlines the standard process for inspecting and replacing serviceable filters on TitanX equipment platforms.

Routine filter replacement helps maintain equipment reliability, reduce contamination, and improve overall system performance.

Service Objectives​

Filter replacement procedures are intended to:

  • Reduce contamination buildup
  • Improve equipment efficiency
  • Protect internal components
  • Maintain airflow and fluid quality
  • Reduce operational failures

Supported Filter Types​

TitanX equipment platforms may include:

  • Engine oil filters
  • Hydraulic filters
  • Fuel filters
  • Air intake filters
  • Cabin air filters

Service intervals may vary depending on operational workload and environmental conditions.

Required Safety Precautions​

Before performing service:

  • Park equipment on stable terrain
  • Shut down equipment fully
  • Engage parking brake
  • Allow systems to cool when required
  • Wear required PPE
warning

Do not remove pressurized filters while equipment systems remain active or under pressure.

Required PPE​

Technicians performing filter replacement must wear:

  • Safety glasses
  • Protective gloves
  • Steel-toe footwear

Additional PPE may be required depending on fluid exposure conditions.

Required Materials​

Verify availability of:

  • Approved TitanX replacement filters
  • Clean service tools
  • Drain containers if required
  • Shop towels or absorbent material
  • Approved disposal containers

Service Procedure​

1. Identify Filter Type and Location​

Verify the correct filter requiring replacement.

Inspect the service area and confirm:

  • Correct equipment model
  • Correct filter type
  • Proper replacement part availability

Do not install incorrect filter components.

2. Prepare Service Area​

Inspect surrounding components for:

  • Active leaks
  • Excessive contamination
  • Damaged hoses or fittings
  • Loose hardware

Clean service areas before removing filters to reduce contamination risk.

3. Remove Existing Filter​

Carefully remove the existing filter according to equipment service specifications.

Inspect removed filters for:

  • Excessive debris buildup
  • Metal contamination
  • Abnormal fluid residue
  • Structural damage

Document abnormal findings when required.

4. Inspect Mounting Surfaces​

Before installation:

  • Verify sealing surfaces are clean
  • Remove old gasket material if present
  • Inspect mounting threads and fittings
  • Confirm no visible damage exists

Improper sealing may cause leaks or pressure loss.

5. Install Replacement Filter​

Install approved TitanX replacement filters according to service specifications.

Verify:

  • Proper alignment
  • Correct seal placement
  • Secure installation
  • No cross-threading

Do not overtighten filter components.

6. Verify System Operation​

After installation:

  • Restart equipment when applicable
  • Monitor for active leaks
  • Verify stable system pressure
  • Review warning indicators
  • Confirm normal equipment operation

Stop operation immediately if abnormal conditions occur.

Service Completion​

After replacement:

  • Dispose of used filters according to environmental requirements
  • Clean surrounding service areas
  • Document service completion
  • Record maintenance intervals

Escalation Guidance​

Escalate equipment for advanced service if:

  • Contamination repeatedly reoccurs
  • Pressure instability continues
  • Active leaks remain present
  • Warning indicators remain active
  • Internal component damage is suspected

Do not return equipment to operation during unresolved fault conditions.
