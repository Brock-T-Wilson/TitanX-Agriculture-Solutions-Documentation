# Hydraulic Fluid Replacement

This procedure outlines the standard process for replacing hydraulic fluid on TitanX equipment platforms.

Proper hydraulic fluid maintenance is required to maintain hydraulic pressure stability, attachment performance, and overall equipment reliability.

## Service Objectives

Hydraulic fluid replacement is intended to:

- Maintain hydraulic system performance
- Reduce internal component wear
- Remove contaminated fluid
- Improve pressure consistency
- Reduce hydraulic system failure risk

## Required Safety Precautions

Before performing service:

- Park equipment on stable terrain
- Shut down equipment fully
- Engage parking brake
- Lower all attachments safely
- Depressurize hydraulic systems
- Wear required PPE

:::warning
Hydraulic systems may remain pressurized after shutdown. Always release stored hydraulic pressure before disconnecting hoses or servicing hydraulic components.
:::

## Required PPE

Technicians performing hydraulic service must wear:

- Safety glasses
- Protective gloves
- Steel-toe footwear
- Protective clothing appropriate for hydraulic fluid handling

## Required Materials

Verify availability of:

- Approved TitanX hydraulic fluid
- Replacement hydraulic filters
- Drain containers
- Clean service funnels
- Shop towels or absorbent material
- Approved disposal containers

## Service Procedure

### 1. Prepare Equipment for Service

Verify:

- Equipment is fully shut down
- Hydraulic systems are depressurized
- Attachments are lowered completely
- Service area is clear of hazards

Inspect the surrounding area for active leaks before beginning service.

---

### 2. Locate Hydraulic Reservoir

Identify the hydraulic reservoir and verify:

- Reservoir access points are clean
- No contamination is present near fill areas
- Reservoir surfaces are free of debris

Prevent contamination from entering hydraulic systems during service.

---

### 3. Drain Existing Hydraulic Fluid

Position approved drain containers beneath service points.

Carefully remove drain plugs and allow hydraulic fluid to drain fully.

Inspect drained fluid for:

- Metal contamination
- Burnt odor
- Water contamination
- Abnormal discoloration

Document abnormal fluid conditions for maintenance records.

---

### 4. Replace Hydraulic Filters

Remove and replace hydraulic filters according to TitanX service specifications.

Verify:

- Correct filter installation
- Proper seal alignment
- No visible damage to filter mounts

Do not reuse contaminated hydraulic filters.

---

### 5. Refill Hydraulic Reservoir

Refill the hydraulic reservoir using approved TitanX hydraulic fluid.

Verify fluid levels according to equipment specifications.

Do not overfill hydraulic systems.

---

### 6. Inspect for Leaks

After refill:

- Inspect all fittings and service points
- Verify drain plugs are secure
- Inspect hydraulic hoses and connections
- Confirm no active leaks exist

---

### 7. Verify Hydraulic Operation

Restart equipment and monitor:

- Hydraulic pressure stability
- Attachment response
- Warning indicators
- Abnormal noise or vibration

Allow hydraulic systems to stabilize before returning equipment to operation.

## Service Completion

After service:

- Dispose of used hydraulic fluid according to environmental requirements
- Document service completion
- Record fluid replacement interval
- Report unresolved hydraulic concerns

## Escalation Guidance

Escalate equipment for advanced service if:

- Hydraulic pressure instability continues
- Active leaks remain present
- Fluid contamination reoccurs
- Warning indicators remain active
- Abnormal hydraulic noise continues

Do not operate equipment during unresolved hydraulic fault conditions.
