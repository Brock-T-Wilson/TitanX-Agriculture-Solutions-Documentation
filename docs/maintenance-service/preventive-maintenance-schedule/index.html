Preventive Maintenance Schedule

Preventive maintenance procedures help maintain equipment reliability, reduce operational downtime, and improve long-term performance across TitanX equipment platforms.

Operators and service personnel must complete all required maintenance intervals according to operational usage and equipment conditions.

Maintenance Objectives​

Preventive maintenance is intended to:

  • Reduce equipment failures
  • Improve operational reliability
  • Extend equipment lifespan
  • Identify developing component issues
  • Maintain safe operating conditions
  • Reduce unplanned downtime

Required Safety Precautions​

Before performing maintenance:

  • Park equipment on stable terrain
  • Shut down equipment fully
  • Engage parking brake
  • Lower all attachments safely
  • Depressurize hydraulic systems
  • Wear required PPE
warning

Always follow lockout/tagout and hydraulic depressurization procedures before performing equipment maintenance.

Daily Maintenance Requirements​

Complete the following inspections before daily operation:

Inspection ItemRequirement
Engine OilVerify acceptable level
Hydraulic FluidInspect level and condition
Fuel SystemInspect for leaks or contamination
Tires / TracksInspect for wear or damage
PTO ComponentsVerify secure operation
Warning IndicatorsConfirm no active critical faults

50-Hour Maintenance Interval​

Perform the following procedures every 50 operational hours:

  • Inspect hydraulic hoses and fittings
  • Verify battery terminal condition
  • Inspect PTO driveline components
  • Lubricate approved grease points
  • Inspect air filtration systems
  • Verify attachment hardware security

100-Hour Maintenance Interval​

Perform the following procedures every 100 operational hours:

  • Replace engine oil and oil filter
  • Inspect hydraulic pressure performance
  • Verify cooling system operation
  • Inspect belts and pulleys
  • Inspect electrical wiring connections
  • Review fault history logs

250-Hour Maintenance Interval​

Perform the following procedures every 250 operational hours:

  • Replace hydraulic filters
  • Inspect transmission components
  • Flush and replace hydraulic fluid if required
  • Inspect steering system operation
  • Verify navigation system calibration
  • Inspect suspension and chassis hardware

Annual Maintenance Requirements​

Complete annual service procedures including:

  • Full hydraulic inspection
  • Electrical system inspection
  • PTO operational testing
  • Cooling system service
  • Structural inspection
  • Operational safety verification

Annual maintenance should be completed by qualified TitanX service personnel.

Maintenance Documentation Requirements​

Operators and technicians should document:

  • Completed service procedures
  • Replacement components
  • Active operational concerns
  • Fluid replacement intervals
  • Inspection failures
  • Escalated service issues

Escalation Guidance​

Escalate equipment for advanced service if:

  • Active leaks are identified
  • Fault indicators remain active
  • Hydraulic instability continues
  • Structural damage is detected
  • Electrical faults remain unresolved

Do not return unsafe equipment to operation.
