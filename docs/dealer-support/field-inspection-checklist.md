# Field Inspection Checklist

This checklist outlines the standard field inspection process for TitanX equipment platforms during operational review, maintenance evaluation, warranty assessment, or dealer service inspection activities.

Field inspections help identify operational concerns, equipment damage, safety hazards, and maintenance requirements before equipment returns to service.

## Inspection Objectives

Field inspections are intended to:

- Verify equipment operating condition
- Identify safety concerns
- Detect hydraulic or mechanical damage
- Support maintenance planning
- Improve operational reliability
- Standardize dealer inspection procedures

## Required Safety Precautions

Before performing field inspections:

- Park equipment on stable terrain
- Shut down equipment fully
- Engage parking brake
- Lower attachments safely
- Wear required PPE

:::warning
Do not inspect active hydraulic, PTO, or moving mechanical systems during equipment operation.
:::

## Required PPE

Personnel performing inspections must wear:

- Safety glasses
- Protective gloves
- Steel-toe footwear
- High-visibility vest during field operations

Additional PPE may be required depending on operational conditions.

## Inspection Checklist

### Equipment Identification

Verify:

- Equipment model
- Serial number
- Operational hour reading
- Equipment configuration

---

### Exterior Inspection

Inspect for:

- Structural damage
- Loose hardware
- Fluid leaks
- Damaged hoses or fittings
- Tire or track wear
- Attachment condition

Document all visible damage.

---

### Hydraulic System Inspection

Verify:

- Hydraulic fluid levels
- Hose condition
- Pressure warning indicators
- Leak-free connections
- Attachment response

Do not operate equipment with unresolved hydraulic leaks.

---

### PTO & Driveline Inspection

Inspect:

- PTO shafts
- Protective shields
- Attachment alignment
- Driveline condition
- Engagement controls

Immediately escalate damaged PTO components.

---

### Electrical System Inspection

Verify:

- Battery condition
- Electrical connections
- Operational displays
- Warning indicators
- Lighting systems

Document active electrical warnings.

---

### Operational Function Test

When safe to perform:

- Verify startup operation
- Test steering response
- Confirm brake functionality
- Verify attachment operation
- Review navigation system status

Stop testing immediately if unsafe conditions occur.

---

### Safety Inspection

Confirm:

- PPE compliance
- Safety decals remain visible
- Emergency shutdown controls function properly
- Fire extinguisher availability when applicable
- Lockout/tagout procedures are followed during service

---

## Inspection Documentation Requirements

Record:

- Inspection date
- Inspector name
- Equipment status
- Active operational concerns
- Required service actions
- Escalated issues

Accurate documentation supports service planning and warranty review.

## Escalation Guidance

Immediately escalate:

- Structural equipment damage
- Hydraulic rupture conditions
- Active electrical hazards
- Brake or steering instability
- Fire-related damage
- Repeated operational failures

Do not return unsafe equipment to operation.
