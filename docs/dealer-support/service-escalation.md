# Service Escalation

This procedure outlines the standard escalation process for unresolved equipment issues, recurring failures, and advanced technical concerns requiring additional TitanX support involvement.

Dealer service teams and field technicians must follow approved escalation procedures when equipment conditions exceed standard troubleshooting or repair capabilities.

## Process Objectives

Service escalation procedures are intended to:

- Improve communication between support teams
- Reduce operational downtime
- Standardize advanced issue handling
- Support timely equipment resolution
- Improve documentation consistency

## Conditions Requiring Escalation

Escalate equipment issues when any of the following occur:

- Repeated startup failures
- Hydraulic pressure instability
- Unresolved PTO engagement failures
- Electrical system faults
- Structural equipment damage
- Navigation system instability
- Safety-related operational concerns
- Repeated warranty-related failures

## Required Escalation Information

Before escalation, dealers and technicians must collect:

| Required Information | Description |
|---|---|
| Equipment Model | TitanX equipment platform |
| Serial Number | Equipment serial identification |
| Operational Hours | Current equipment hours |
| Failure Description | Detailed issue summary |
| Diagnostic Findings | Completed troubleshooting results |
| Active Warning Indicators | Current system alerts |
| Photos / Attachments | Supporting evidence when applicable |

Incomplete escalation requests may delay support response.

## Service Escalation Procedure

### 1. Perform Initial Troubleshooting

Complete all applicable troubleshooting procedures before escalation.

Verify:

- Inspection procedures were completed
- Maintenance requirements were reviewed
- Active fault indicators were documented
- Basic operational checks were performed

Do not escalate issues without completing required diagnostic steps.

---

### 2. Document Operational Conditions

Record:

- Equipment operating environment
- Failure conditions
- Frequency of occurrence
- Environmental factors
- Operator observations
- Maintenance history

Accurate operational information improves escalation efficiency.

---

### 3. Identify Safety Concerns

Immediately identify any:

- Hydraulic hazards
- Electrical hazards
- Structural damage
- Fire risk conditions
- Unsafe equipment movement
- Environmental hazards

Unsafe equipment must be removed from service immediately.

---

### 4. Submit Escalation Request

Submit escalation documentation through approved TitanX support channels.

Verify:

- Required information is complete
- Diagnostic findings are attached
- Photos and supporting documentation are included
- Active safety concerns are identified clearly

---

### 5. Await Technical Review

TitanX support personnel may:

- Request additional diagnostics
- Approve component replacement
- Recommend additional inspection procedures
- Escalate the issue to engineering review
- Dispatch field support assistance

Do not perform unauthorized repairs during active escalation review.

---

### 6. Document Resolution Actions

After escalation resolution:

- Record repair procedures completed
- Document replacement components
- Update service history records
- Verify operational functionality
- Confirm safety requirements are met

## Emergency Escalation Conditions

Immediately escalate emergency conditions including:

- Hydraulic rupture
- Fire or smoke conditions
- Structural failure
- Brake or steering failure
- Electrical arcing
- Operator injury

Emergency hazards require immediate operational shutdown.
